Research

Bridging AI advances with transformative real-world applications

Research Overview

My research focuses on building intelligent, human-centered systems at the intersection of computer vision, natural language processing, and human–computer interaction. I develop multimodal AI models that integrate visual, linguistic, and motion-based signals to enable natural, interpretable, and accessible interaction between humans and machines.

A central theme of my work is multimodal perception and interaction, spanning sign language technologies, AI-driven human–computer interfaces, human activity understanding, and generative models for expressive human representation. I emphasize real-time systems, robustness in unconstrained environments, and interpretability for safety-critical and accessibility-oriented applications.

Key Contributions

  • Real-time multimodal fingertip contact detection using RGB-based depth and motion fusion (CVPR 2026, Main Conference)
  • VR keyboard systems for natural text input in AR/VR environments
  • Vision-based hand and finger interaction for accessibility and smart interfaces
  • Talking human face generation: survey (Expert Systems with Applications, IF: 9.29)
  • Generative adversarial networks and their application to 3D face generation: a survey (Image and Vision Computing)
  • Personalized emotion visualization and interpretable multimodal fusion for human-centered NLP (ACL 2026, Findings)
  • Research on diffusion models and LLMs for sign language synthesis
