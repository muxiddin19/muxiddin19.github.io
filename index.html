About Me

I am a dedicated researcher working at the intersection of human–computer interaction and artificial intelligence. My research aims to bridge fundamental advances in AI with transformative real‑world applications, with particular emphasis on multimodal sign language technologies, human activity recognition, and generative AI for natural and accessible human interaction.

I received my PhD in Smart Engineering from Inha University in 2025, where my doctoral research, titled “Advancing Sign Language Recognition: A Multimodal Deep Learning Framework with Keypoint Vectorization,” focused on developing robust and scalable multimodal learning frameworks. My work integrates deep learning, computer vision, and human–computer interaction to enhance accessibility and improve human–machine communication.

I have held diverse research appointments across leading institutions. I currently serve as a Research Professor in the Department of Computer Engineering at Gachon University, where my research focuses on multimodal learning, Transformers, diffusion models, and LLM‑based agents. In parallel, I work as a Senior Researcher at the Voice AI Research Institute of Inha University, conducting research on multimodal sign language recognition using Vision Transformers, diffusion models, and large language models. Previously, I was a Postdoctoral Researcher at KAIST’s SpaceTop Research Center (ITRC), contributing to a VR keyboard project aimed at accurate fingertip detection and tracking. Earlier in my career, I worked as a Research Assistant at Inha University, supporting AI and medical AI courses and supervising student research projects.

In addition to my research activities, I have extensive teaching experience at universities in Korea and Uzbekistan, delivering undergraduate and graduate courses in deep learning, computer vision, cybersecurity, machine learning, numerical analysis, and mathematical modeling. I am currently an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Engineering at Gachon University, where I lead research in the Intelligent Network & Embedded Systems Lab. My ongoing work centers on multimodal sign language recognition, Vision Transformers, diffusion models, and large language models, with broader interests in advanced AI‑driven perception and understanding.

Research Focus Areas

Sign Language Technologies

Real-time translation, cross-linguistic corpora, multimodal recognition systems, and wearable sensor integration for accessible communication.

Human Activity Recognition

Doppler radar analysis, privacy-preserving surveillance, skeleton-based pose estimation, and healthcare monitoring applications.

Generative AI

3D facial animation, speech-to-face synthesis, GANs, diffusion models, and talking human face generation systems.

AI-driven HCI

Hand and finger detection, virtual keyboards, AR/VR/XR interfaces, smart glasses, and natural interaction paradigms.
